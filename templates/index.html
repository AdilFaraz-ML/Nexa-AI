Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about IUB and Nexa AI. Can't find an answer? Chat with the bot above.

What is Nexa AI? +
Nexa AI is the AI-powered chatbot of Islamia University of Bahawalpur (IUB). It answers queries about transport, scholarships, admissions, general queries, and more. It is developed by Adil Faraz as a Final Year Project (FYP) at IUB.
How can I obtain a library card? +
To obtain a library card at The Islamia University of Bahawalpur (IUB), you are required to visit the Sir Sadiq Library in person. You must present your official student ID card for registration. After completing the registration process, a library card will be issued to you. This card allows you to access both digital and physical library resources. Please note that in case of loss or damage to any borrowed book, you will be required to compensate for it in accordance with the university's library policies.
What are the university transport timings? +
The university transport service operates according to the following schedule:
  1. Buses run from 7:30 AM to 6:30 PM.
  2. Buses typically arrive at intervals of 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  3. These timings may vary during examination periods.
How can I contact IUB administration? +
You can contact IUB at +92-62-9250235 or visit the main campus at Bahawalpur.
Which documents are required for admission? +
For admission to IUB, you are required to submit the following documents:
  1. Verified result cards of Matric (10th) and Intermediate (12th).
  2. CNIC or B-Form.
  3. Recent passport-size photographs.
  4. Any additional documents (if required by the university).
All documents must be submitted along with an affidavit at the IUB Old Campus for verification purposes.
How can I download my transcript? +
To download your transcript, follow these steps:
  1. Sign in to your My-IUB account.
  2. On the homepage, navigate to the Transcript tab.
  3. Click on the Download PDF button to download your transcript.
What is LMS? +
The Learning Management System (LMS) is an online platform provided by IUB through its E-portal. It is used by instructors to upload lectures, assignments, and course materials. Students are required to submit their assignments through this platform within the given deadlines. LMS can be accessed through the My-IUB portal.

How to create an account on the IUB E-portal? +
To create an account on the IUB E-portal, follow these steps:

  2. Open the official portal website.

How to apply for university hostel? +
The hostel application process differs based on your current semester:

1st Semester Students:
  1. Visit the Abbasia Campus at the time of your admission.
  2. Apply for hostel accommodation from the Admission Department.
  3. Prepare and submit: paid fee challan of Rs. 20,000 (per semester), attested affidavit, and CNIC copy.
  4. Upload all required documents on the IUB E-portal.
2nd Semester or Above:
  1. Visit the Executive Hostel at Baghdad-ul-Jadeed (BJC) Campus in person.
  2. Submit a physical application along with: paid fee challan of Rs. 20,000 (per semester), attested affidavit, and CNIC copy.
  3. Upload all required documents on the IUB E-portal.
  4. Accommodation will be allotted subject to availability.
How to apply for a scholarship from the portal? +
To apply for a scholarship through the IUB E-portal, follow these steps:
  1. Sign in to your My-IUB account.
  2. Navigate to the Scholarship tab from the menu.
  3. Fill in the required information and upload the necessary documents.

If awarded, the scholarship amount will reflect on your portal and will be deducted from your fee challan in the following semester.

How can I apply for course repeat or improvement? +
To apply for a course repeat or improvement, follow these steps:
  1. Log in to your My-IUB account.
  2. Navigate to the Course Repeat Challan tab.
  3. Select either Course Repeat or Course Improvement.
  4. Choose the subject(s) from your semester list.
  5. Download the fee challan.
  6. Pay the required fee.
  7. Submit the paid challan copy to the relevant clerk.
After submission, you will be assigned to a class for the selected course. Please note that the fee for course repeat is generally higher than the fee for course improvement.
Is full fee payment required after selection in the merit list? +
Yes, once your name appears in the IUB merit list, you are required to pay the full admission fee initially. Afterward, semester fees may be paid in two installments, subject to university policy.
How can I apply for fee installment payment? +
To apply for fee installment, follow these steps:
  1. Log in to your My-IUB account.
  2. Navigate to the Financial Voucher tab.
  3. Select the challan for which you want to apply for installments.
  4. Click on the Installment Option (usually highlighted in orange).
  5. Submit your request.
Please note that this facility is available for a limited time each semester.

How can I apply for the National Aptitude Test (NAT)? +
To apply for the NAT, follow these steps:
  1. Register on the IUB E-portal (if you do not already have an account).
  2. Log in to your account.
  3. Navigate to the NAT application section.
  4. Fill out the required details.
  5. Generate and download the test challan (approximately Rs. 1,000).
  6. Pay the challan fee at the designated bank.
  7. Upload the paid challan receipt on the portal.
After verification, you will be issued a roll number slip along with the test date.

How to apply for a scholarship? +
The application procedure varies depending on the scholarship type:

Departmental Scholarships (Kinship, Needy Fee Remission, Orphan, Hafiz-e-Quran, Position Holder):
  1. Collect and fill out the application form in hard copy.
  2. Attach the required documents including an income certificate.
  3. Submit the form in person to the designated scholarship coordinator in your department.

International Scholarships (US, UK, Scotland, etc.):
  1. The department or official university social media will share the relevant application link.
  2. Visit the provided link and complete the application on the respective scholarship website.

How to apply for clearance (8th semester students)? +
Students who have completed all degree requirements must complete the clearance process to receive their final result card or transcript.

Step 1 — Generate and Pay Challans:
  1. Log in to the IUB E-portal and go to Generate Online Challan.
  2. Search for "Alumni" and generate the Alumni Challan (Rs. 500).
  3. Search for "Degree", select Normal Degree Fee, and generate the challan (Rs. 5,000).
  4. Pay both challans via HBL App, JazzCash, Women Bank, or any HBL branch.
Step 2 — Upload Documents & Apply for Clearance:
  1. Log in to your My-IUB portal and go to Menu → My Documents.
  2. Upload scanned documents in JPG/PNG format.
  3. From the same menu, go to My Clearance and click Apply for Clearance.
  4. Enter remarks as "Degree Completed" and submit.
  5. Wait 24–48 hours for IT Department verification.
Step 3 — Obtain Physical Signatures (in this order):
  1. Library Office
  2. Security Office (Main Gate)
  3. Alumni Office (Taabish Alwari Building)
  4. Chairman (Department Office)
  5. Accounts Branch (Old / Abbasia Campus)
Step 4 — Submit & Collect Result Card:
  1. Submit the signed clearance form to the Department Office.
  2. Once result cards are announced, visit the department in person with your clearance form to collect it.
Note: Only the concerned student is permitted to collect their own result card.
If I score less in course improvement, which marks will be considered? +
If you attempt a course improvement and score lower than your previous attempt, your original (higher) marks will be retained. The new lower marks will not replace the previous result.
